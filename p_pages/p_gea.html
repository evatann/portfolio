Stevia Consumption and Student Mental Health Analysis

Jan 2025 – Apr 2025

Overview

Stevia Consumption and Student Mental Health Analysis is a statistics and data analysis project that evaluates both experimental research and large-scale observational data. The first component critically reviews a randomized controlled trial investigating the effects of daily stevia consumption over 12 weeks on glucose homeostasis, body weight, and energy intake in healthy adults with normal BMI.

Through hypothesis testing and interpretation of statistical results, the project assesses whether stevia consumption significantly affects metabolic outcomes. The analysis shows that while stevia did not significantly affect glucose metabolism, it was associated with weight maintenance and reduced energy intake compared to the control group. The study design, including its randomized controlled structure, baseline characteristics, and sampling approach, was also evaluated to identify strengths such as controlled experimentation and limitations such as small sample size and lack of blinding.

The second component focuses on data analysis of a large dataset containing nearly 20,000 student records. The dataset was cleaned and prepared by identifying anomalies, removing invalid entries, and classifying variables. Using descriptive statistics, scatter plots, boxplots, and regression analysis, relationships between study hours, academic pressure, and CGPA were explored.

Inferential statistical methods including confidence interval estimation and chi-squared tests were applied to investigate mental health patterns among students. The analysis found a high proportion of students reporting depression, and identified significant associations between sleep duration, financial stress, and mental well-being. Overall, the project demonstrates the application of statistical methods to evaluate research studies, analyze real-world datasets, and derive insights relevant to public health and student well-being.

Overall, this project strengthened my ability to apply statistical reasoning, evaluate experimental research, analyze large datasets, and communicate data-driven insights. It also emphasized the importance of data quality, ethical interpretation of results, and the societal implications of health and education research.

Key Highlights

➤Experimental Study Evaluation: Critically analysed a randomized controlled trial investigating the effects of daily stevia consumption on glucose homeostasis, body weight, and energy intake.
➤Hypothesis Testing & Statistical Interpretation: Interpreted p-values, null and alternative hypotheses, and statistical significance to evaluate outcomes related to metabolism, weight change, and dietary intake.
➤Large Dataset Analysis: Cleaned and analyzed a dataset of nearly 20,000 student observations, identifying anomalies, removing invalid entries, and preparing variables for statistical analysis.
➤Data Visualization & Regression: Used scatter plots, boxplots, and linear regression to examine relationships between study hours, academic pressure, and CGPA.
➤Inferential Statistics: Applied confidence intervals, correlation analysis, and chi-squared tests to investigate associations between sleep duration, depression, and other lifestyle factors.
➤Socioeconomic Insights: Explored the relationship between financial stress and student mental well-being, identifying significant associations with depression and suicidal thoughts.
➤Skills Developed: Strengthened statistical analysis, data cleaning, hypothesis testing, and evidence-based interpretation using tools such as Excel and Radiant.
