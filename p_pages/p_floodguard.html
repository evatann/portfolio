FloodGuard: MRT Station Flood Resilience Enhancement - Project Planning Study

Sep 2025 – Nov 2025

Overview

As part of a Project Management and Finance module at the National University of Singapore, I collaborated with a team to develop an innovative project management proposal addressing flood resilience challenges in Singapore’s land transport infrastructure. The project focused on identifying flood risks and independently designing solutions to enhance protection for five hypothetical flood-prone underground MRT stations, applying principles from the PMI PMBOK framework.

Our team conceptualised engineering and operational mitigation strategies, including modular flood barriers, automated pumping systems, waterproofing upgrades, real-time environmental monitoring, emergency operating procedures, and staff training programmes. These solutions were designed after analysing vulnerabilities and integrating practical, implementable measures to improve resilience of urban rail infrastructure.

We developed a comprehensive project management plan spanning all PMBOK knowledge areas: integration, scope, time, cost, quality, risk, resource, communications, and procurement management. Deliverables included a Project Charter, a 48-week Work Breakdown Structure, detailed Gantt charts with critical path analysis, and multi-year cost projections totalling approximately $10.08 million, incorporating contingency planning for unforeseen events.

Structured risk analysis was conducted, identifying over fifteen potential technical, operational, and regulatory risks, along with quality assurance processes aligned with ISO 9001:2015 and guidelines from the Land Transport Authority and Public Utilities Board. Weekly presentations were delivered to instructors and peers, demonstrating progress, justifying design choices, and refining project plans based on feedback.

This project strengthened my skills in innovative problem-solving, infrastructure project planning, stakeholder coordination, financial analysis, risk management, and structured project management methodologies, while providing practical experience in designing resilient solutions for urban transport systems under real-world constraints.
