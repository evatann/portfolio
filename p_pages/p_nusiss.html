Engineering Digital Innovation

Project Genesis

Sep 2022 – Oct 2022

Overview

As part of the Digital Futurist Challenge organized by NUS-ISS for its 40th anniversary, my team envisioned Singapore’s healthcare landscape in 2060 and designed Project Genesis, a compact, AI-powered predictive healthcare system aimed at preventing chronic diseases and improving accessibility, affordability, and patient well-being.

In 2060, chronic diseases are expected to rise due to an aging population, and healthcare costs will increase with advanced technologies. Project Genesis addresses these challenges by using AI and predictive analytics to monitor users’ vital signs—including cholesterol, blood glucose, ECG, SPO2, and bone density—and provide early warnings and actionable health guidance. We prototyped key functions using an Arduino-based system to simulate data collection, device interaction, and predictive outputs, demonstrating feasibility of the concept in a tangible model.

The system emphasizes personalized, accessible, and decentralized healthcare, allowing users to interact with a small device that is compassionate and efficient. Additional features include a virtual AI chatbot for psychological support and the potential for wearable integration to provide continuous, versatile healthcare monitoring.

Project Genesis is designed with sustainability in mind, using recyclable materials for the casing and energy-efficient rechargeable batteries. By decentralizing healthcare, minimizing resource consumption, and focusing on prevention, the system reduces hospital overcrowding, supports doctors, and improves population health outcomes.

This project strengthened my future thinking, design thinking, strategic planning, embedded prototyping, and digital innovation skills, while allowing me to explore the potential impact of AI-driven predictive healthcare technologies on society, accessibility, and environmental sustainability.
