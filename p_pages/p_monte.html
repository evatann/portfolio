Monte Carlo Simulation and Reliability Analysis

Oct 2025 – Nov 2025

Overview

This project explored the use of Monte Carlo simulation to study the behavior, performance, and reliability of engineering systems under uncertainty. The aim was to understand how variability in system parameters—such as loads, dimensions, and material properties—affects overall system performance and failure risk.

The project involved generating random samples from both normal and lognormal distributions and comparing empirical statistics to theoretical expectations. I investigated how the choice of distribution influences system reliability, particularly for skewed lognormal variables which tend to increase the probability of failure compared to symmetric normal variables.

Practical applications included reliability analysis of components and systems under uncertain load and capacity conditions. One example studied the probability of component failure using combinations of normal and lognormal variables. A more complex example involved probabilistic analysis of beam tip displacement, where uncertainties in applied loads, cross-sectional dimensions, and material modulus were incorporated to predict performance and failure risk.

This project strengthened my skills in statistical analysis, Monte Carlo simulation, probabilistic modeling, reliability assessment, and interpretation of uncertainty impacts on engineering design. It provided hands-on experience in applying probabilistic methods to real-world engineering problems, improving my understanding of variability-driven risk assessment in complex systems.

Key Highlights

➤Monte Carlo Simulation: Used random sampling to evaluate system behavior under uncertainty.
➤Statistical Analysis: Compared empirical statistics to theoretical expectations for normal and lognormal distributions.
➤Reliability Assessment: Calculated probability of failure for components and systems under variable loads and capacities.
➤Probabilistic Modeling: Modeled complex engineering systems, including beam displacement under uncertain parameters.
➤Impact of Uncertainty: Examined how distribution types and variability affect system reliability and design safety.
➤Skills Developed: Strengthened Monte Carlo simulation, probabilistic analysis, statistical reasoning, and engineering risk assessment.
