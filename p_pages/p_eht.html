Ethical Analysis of Emerging Healthcare Technologies

Sep 2025 – Oct 2025

Overview

I collaborated with a small team to conduct an in-depth ethical analysis of emerging healthcare technologies, applying core bioethical principles—autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice, and privacy—to evaluate societal, regulatory, and clinical implications of rapidly advancing medical innovations.

The project focused on four key technologies: CRISPR Genome Editing, Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare Diagnostics, Brain–Computer Interfaces, and Organ Replacement Technologies. For each technology, we examined potential risks and benefits, addressing governance challenges such as genetic equity, algorithmic bias, neural data privacy, and ethical patient selection, alongside broader issues like regulatory frameworks, access to care, and implications for vulnerable populations.

As a team, we developed evidence-based ethical frameworks and policy recommendations, including proposals for international governance in genome editing, bias auditing and transparency standards for AI diagnostics, safeguards for neural data privacy in brain–computer interfaces, and ethical oversight for organ replacement technologies.

The project culminated in a comparative ethical matrix and case-based risk analysis highlighting shared and technology-specific ethical concerns, providing actionable insights for policymakers, clinicians, and technology developers. This experience strengthened our understanding of the intersection between engineering innovation, healthcare policy, and bioethics, emphasizing the importance of integrating ethical considerations into advanced medical technology development and deployment.
