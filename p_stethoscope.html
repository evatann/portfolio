Engineering Signal Processing

Acoustic Stethoscope Signal Processing System

May 2022 – Sep 2022

Overview

I designed and implemented a biomedical signal processing system to amplify and filter heart and lung sounds for clearer audio output, demonstrating the application of analogue electronics in medical signal acquisition. The system consisted of a microphone input stage, a non-inverting operational amplifier preamplifier, a low-pass filter, and a final audio amplification stage with an adjustable line-out connected to a speaker for real-time sound monitoring.

The project began with the design and simulation of analogue circuits, where key parameters such as input voltage (Vin), output voltage (Vout), gain, and voltage gain in decibels (Av = 20·log(|Vout/Vin|)) were calculated and recorded to evaluate circuit performance. Frequency response analysis was conducted by plotting measured values to determine the cutoff frequency of the low-pass filter and ensure unwanted high-frequency noise was attenuated while preserving the biomedical signals.

Following the design phase, the system was prototyped on a breadboard and tested using laboratory instruments including an oscilloscope and multimeter. Test points were implemented at different stages of the circuit to verify each component block individually, enabling systematic troubleshooting and adjustments to component values for accurate signal amplification and filtering.

Through iterative testing and validation, the circuit was optimized to produce clear and stable audio reproduction of biological sounds. The project received an A grade and strengthened my skills in analogue circuit design, biomedical signal processing, frequency response analysis, and hardware debugging.
