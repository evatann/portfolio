Parking Lot Occupancy Counter – Digital Logic System

Nov 2021 – Jan 2022

Overview

As part of the Digital Fundamentals module at Temasek Polytechnic, I developed a functional parking lot occupancy counter using digital logic circuits and integrated components to simulate a real-world automated parking management system. The project applied theoretical concepts from digital electronics to create a practical solution for tracking vehicle entry and exit in a parking facility.

The system was designed using up/down counter logic implemented with digital ICs to track parking occupancy with a maximum capacity of 40 vehicles. A card reader or slider mechanism was used to simulate vehicle entry and exit, incrementing or decrementing the counter accordingly to represent gate sensor activity. An overflow detection circuit was also implemented to trigger a buzzer alert when the parking lot reached full capacity.

The circuit was assembled on a breadboard with proper IC connections, power distribution, and signal routing. I also incorporated a push-button reset interface that allowed users to acknowledge alerts and silence the buzzer. Throughout the development process, I applied concepts such as registers and counter circuits, while debugging the system through systematic troubleshooting and testing multiple scenarios to ensure reliable operation.

TThis project strengthened my understanding of digital logic design, counter circuits, and integrated circuit interfacing, while demonstrating how fundamental digital components can be combined to create practical automation solutions.

Key Highlights

🛠️Counter Logic Design: Implemented up/down counters using digital ICs to monitor parking occupancy for up to 40 vehicles.
📊Sensor Simulation: Used card reader/slider mechanisms to detect vehicle entry (increment) and exit (decrement), mimicking real gate sensors.
🎯Alert System: Designed overflow detection logic that triggers a buzzer when maximum capacity (40 vehicles) is reached.
🎯User Control Interface: Incorporated push-button reset functionality to acknowledge alerts and silence the buzzer.
🎯Circuit Assembly: Constructed the system on a breadboard, ensuring proper IC connections, stable power distribution, and signal integrity.
🎯Practical Application: Applied theoretical knowledge of registers, counters, and digital signal processing to create a working automated system.
🎯Debugging & Testing: Performed systematic troubleshooting and tested multiple scenarios to ensure accurate operation and reliability.
